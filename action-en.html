Club of the disabled

This sector of the shelter cares for, rehabilitates and promotes the care of animals that require special care. These are blind, three-legged or paralyzed dogs that need a wheelchair to move around. El Campito is the only shelter in the world that cares for 130 dogs that need wheelchairs and special care to move around. It is through this area that we play an important role in society, not only in raising awareness about adoptions, but also in the dignified treatment and care of dogs. but also in the dignified treatment and rights of the animal, rejecting euthanasia and promoting rehabilitation. Many inhabitants of the club are adopted and have a dignified life full of love next to those who open the doors of their hearts, their homes and integrate them into the family.

Club of the oldies

This section of the Campito Refugio is dedicated to introduce and tell you the stories of our adult and old camperitos. Many of them have arrived at the Campito abandoned by their families just because they are no longer puppies, and many others have lived in the street all their lives, reaching old age without knowing the warmth of a family. We think that someday we will all reach that point and we would like our family to take care of us and accompany us with lots of love. Let us know that adopting an old person is something wonderful. Although it is often thought that it is better to adopt a puppy to grow up with the family, we want to tell you that adults and old dogs have an immense capacity to give and receive love.

The street is not for any dog and even less for an old one.

Our volunteers

Currently about 150 volunteers work in our shelter providing companionship, stimulation and exercise to our animals. Their work is provided in different areas, from reeducation and socialization of animals with behavioral problems to fundraising and collection.
